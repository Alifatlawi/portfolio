## Design Context

### Users

**Primary**: Technical recruiters and engineering managers at product-led companies (especially iOS / mobile-first shops), landing here from LinkedIn, GitHub, or a résumé link. They spend 30–90 seconds deciding whether Ali is worth a conversation.

**Secondary**: Freelance inbound — founders or small teams looking for someone to build or ship an iOS or cross-platform app.

**Viewing context**: Mostly desktop during work hours (initial scan), with mobile follow-up later. They arrive skeptical — every portfolio they see looks the same.

**Job to be done**: "Convince me in one scroll that this person has taste, ships real things, and would be pleasant to work with."

### Brand Personality

Three words: **engineered, considered, quietly confident.**

- **Engineered, not decorated.** Every element should feel load-bearing. If it's not communicating something, it shouldn't be there.
- **Considered, not busy.** Restraint is the signal. Someone who needs to prove they're good covers everything in particles and gradients. Someone who *is* good trusts white space.
- **Quietly confident, not humble.** Strong typographic hierarchy, unapologetic claims, real work front-and-center. No "aspiring developer" energy — two apps are shipped on the App Store.

**Emotional target**: the feeling of opening an Apple press release or a well-designed case study. Calm, deliberate, trust-building.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Commitment**: Modern product studio — editorial density, Swiss-inflected structure, with a single distinctive typographic voice that isn't in the reflex-reject list.

**Specific references (the feel, not the pixels)**:
- Linear's marketing site — restrained palette, confident type, purposeful motion
- Rauno Freiberg's portfolio (rauno.me) — zero decoration, everything is craft signal
- Apple Newsroom — serif/sans pairing, generous space, editorial rhythm
- Teenage Engineering's product pages — engineered feel, intentional whitespace

**Explicit anti-references (the current site is many of these — replace, don't iterate)**:
- ❌ Rainbow gradient text (blue → purple → pink clip-text on every heading)
- ❌ Animated gradient mesh backgrounds
- ❌ Floating decorative emoji, sparkles, and tech icons
- ❌ Glow and neon ring effects around the profile photo
- ❌ Inter / DM Sans / Plus Jakarta / Space Grotesk — the default-font monoculture
- ❌ Glassmorphism and frosted-glass cards
- ❌ Identical rounded-rectangle project cards in a symmetric grid
- ❌ Every heading set to `bg-clip-text` gradient fill (banned by skill)

### Theme

**Light mode as the default**, with a carefully-crafted dark mode as a toggle.

Reasoning: most developer portfolios default to dark "to look cool." A confident light-first portfolio signals taste over reflex, mirrors Apple's own design language (which aligns with the iOS work being shown), and reads better in the context this is actually viewed in — a recruiter's bright office, a hiring manager's daylit laptop. Dark mode stays for the subset of visitors who prefer it.

### Color Palette

Use OKLCH, not HSL. Tint neutrals toward a single restrained brand hue.

**Direction**:
- **Neutrals**: warm off-whites and ink-blacks, faintly tinted toward a single hue (not the current blue — something more distinctive, e.g. a warm terracotta or deep ochre for accent, kept to ~10% of visual weight).
- **Accent**: ONE color, used rarely. No triadic rainbow. No purple gradients.
- **60 / 30 / 10**: dominant neutral surface, secondary text and lines, accent sparingly.

**Avoid**: pure `#000` / `#fff`, the cyan-on-dark AI palette, any gradient that touches three+ hues.

### Typography

**Pairing** (must be picked from outside the reflex-reject list — Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta, Fraunces, Space Grotesk, etc. are all banned):

- **Display**: a distinctive editorial face — candidates: **Söhne**, **GT America**, **Migra**, **ABC Diatype**, **ABC Favorit**, **PP Editorial New**, **PP Neue Montreal**, or a free alternative from Pangram Pangram / Velvetyne / Klim (e.g. **Tiempos** / **National 2** analogs).
- **Body**: a refined humanist sans or warm serif for long-form — not the same family as the display.
- **Mono** (used sparingly for tags, metadata, not decoration): **JetBrains Mono**, **Berkeley Mono**, or **Departure Mono**.

**Scale**: 5 steps, min 1.25 ratio. Fluid sizing with `clamp()` for display headings only. Body stays in fixed `rem`.

### Layout & Space

- 4pt spacing scale with semantic tokens (`--space-sm`, `--space-md`...), not pixel-named.
- Vary spacing deliberately — do not pad every section identically. Use rhythm for hierarchy.
- Body text capped at 65–75ch.
- Break the symmetric grid at least once for emphasis (e.g. the featured project vs. the list, the hero vs. the rest).
- Asymmetric compositions beat centered everything.

### Motion

- One **orchestrated** reveal on initial load (staggered, exponential ease-out).
- Micro-interactions only where they communicate state — link hover, submit, theme toggle.
- Zero continuous looping animations on decorative elements (no perpetual floats, no auto-rotating rings).
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### Accessibility

- WCAG AA as the baseline (AAA for body copy where achievable).
- Every interactive element reachable by keyboard; visible focus rings that match the aesthetic.
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support — critical given how motion-heavy the current version is.
- Color never the sole signal for state.

## Design Principles (guide every decision)

1. **Ship signal, cut noise.** If an element doesn't earn its place, delete it. The current site has ~40 animated decorative elements on the hero alone — 35 of them communicate nothing.
2. **One voice, not five.** One display typeface, one accent color, one motion philosophy. Cohesion over variety.
3. **Trust the work.** The apps on the App Store are the proof — the design's job is to frame them, not to out-shout them.
4. **Restraint is the flex.** On this portfolio, a calm landing page is a stronger claim about craft than any amount of particle animation.
5. **No AI tells.** Gradient text, rainbow meshes, glassmorphism, floating icons, Inter — all absent. If it looks like a 2024–25 AI template, it's wrong.

## What to check before any design work

Before generating or changing UI in this project:
1. Re-read this file.
2. Verify your chosen font is not in the reflex-reject list.
3. Verify you're not reaching for `bg-clip-text` + gradient, `border-left: Npx solid ...`, or a blue→purple→pink palette.
4. Default to *less* — then add only what's load-bearing.
